# Organization Dashboards, Pages, and Flows Concept

Updated: 2026-05-18

## Purpose

I am thinking about the organization workspace as the team version of the product. It should help a business, agency, or marketing team create content together, manage approvals, organize assets, schedule posts, and control who is allowed to do what.

The organization experience should not feel like a personal account with extra pages added. It should feel like a shared operating space where content moves through people, roles, brand rules, review stages, and publishing decisions.

The main problem I am trying to solve is that team content creation is usually scattered. Ideas live in chats, assets live in folders, drafts live in design tools, approvals happen in messages, schedules live in calendars, and publishing happens somewhere else. The organization workspace should bring those parts into one connected flow.

## Core Organization Concept

The organization workspace is built around a few ideas:

- Members have roles.
- Roles control what each person can do.
- Content belongs to an organization, not just one person.
- Brand rules should guide content before it is published.
- Drafts should move through review when needed.
- Approved content should move into a schedule.
- Shared assets should be easy to reuse.
- Team activity should be visible without asking around.

The product should support both small teams and larger organizations. A small team may only need simple collaboration and scheduling. A larger organization may need stricter roles, approvals, credit control, brand projects, and audit visibility.

## Main Workspace Areas

I am thinking about the organization workspace as a set of connected areas, not isolated pages.

The major areas are:

- Overview.
- Workspace dashboard.
- My Office.
- Pipeline.
- Pipeline Tasks.
- Calendar.
- Asset Library.
- Common Room.
- Team Activity.
- Admin Brand Kit.
- Admin Members.
- Admin Roles.
- Admin Pipelines.
- Admin Credits.
- Admin Settings.
- Join and invitation flow.
- Client review flow.

Each page should answer a different team question.

## Overview Dashboard

The Overview dashboard should help the team understand what is happening across the organization.

I am thinking of it as the high-level pulse of the workspace.

It should answer questions like:

- What content is scheduled soon?
- What is waiting for review?
- What has already been approved?
- Where is the team blocked?
- Are there enough reusable assets?
- Are connected accounts healthy?

For admins, the overview should focus more on organization health. For regular members, it should focus more on their work and the content moving through the team.

## Workspace Dashboard

The Workspace dashboard should be the member's daily starting point.

It should help a team member see what needs attention without checking every page manually.

It could show:

- Drafts the member is working on.
- Items waiting for feedback.
- Content that needs revision.
- Approved items ready to schedule.
- Tasks assigned to the member.
- Upcoming schedule pressure.
- Shortcuts to create, schedule, or continue work.

The goal is to make the member's day clear. They should know what to do next as soon as they enter the workspace.

## My Office

My Office should be the member's personal workbench inside the organization.

This is where a member can create or refine org content before it becomes part of the team review process.

I am thinking of My Office as the place for:

- Creating new organization drafts.
- Continuing unfinished drafts.
- Reviewing draft quality before submission.
- Choosing the brand project or campaign context.
- Submitting content into the Pipeline.
- Deleting drafts that are no longer useful.

My Office should feel personal, but the work still belongs to the organization. It is the bridge between individual creation and team workflow.

## Pipeline

The Pipeline should show where content is in the review process.

I am thinking of it as the team's approval board.

It should help the team see:

- What is newly submitted.
- What is in review.
- What needs changes.
- What has been approved.
- What was rejected or withdrawn.
- What is ready to schedule or publish.

The Pipeline should reduce confusion around status. A team member should not need to ask, "Has this been approved yet?" The answer should be visible.

## Pipeline Tasks

Pipeline Tasks should support the work that happens around content.

Some content needs more than approval. It may need copy edits, design updates, asset replacement, legal review, platform checks, or scheduling follow-up.

I am thinking tasks should help the team:

- Assign work to specific people.
- Track due dates.
- Separate content review from operational follow-up.
- See what is blocked.
- Keep the content workflow moving.

Tasks should make the organization workspace feel operational, not just creative.

## Calendar

The Calendar should be the publishing operations surface.

I am thinking of it as the place where approved content becomes a planned schedule.

It should help users:

- See what is scheduled.
- Choose when content should go out.
- Move approved content into available slots.
- Publish immediately when allowed.
- Batch schedule multiple posts.
- View platform and approval status.
- Connect calendar plans back to assets, tasks, and pipeline items.

The Calendar should not just show dates. It should help the team manage publishing pressure and avoid gaps or conflicts.

## Asset Library

The Asset Library should be the organization's shared source of reusable creative material.

It should hold things like:

- Product images.
- Brand visuals.
- Videos.
- Campaign assets.
- Logos.
- Reference files.
- Approved design materials.
- Reusable media for posts.

The Asset Library should help teams stop searching through random folders and chat threads. Assets should be organized, searchable, reusable, and connected to the content they support.

I am also thinking the library should support folders, permissions, asset movement, and links between assets and posts.

## Common Room

The Common Room should be the organization's collaboration space.

I am thinking of it as a shared room where the team can discuss content, campaigns, assets, pipeline items, and decisions.

It could support:

- Team channels.
- Campaign discussions.
- Asset references.
- Pipeline references.
- AI-assisted team conversation.
- Project-specific rooms.

The Common Room should reduce the need to leave the product just to ask questions or coordinate content decisions.

## Team Activity

Team Activity should show what has recently happened across the organization.

It should help answer:

- Who submitted something?
- Who approved or requested changes?
- What was scheduled?
- What changed in the pipeline?
- What assets were added?
- What tasks moved forward?

The idea is to create a lightweight activity trail so people can catch up quickly without asking the team for status updates.

## Admin Brand Kit

The Admin Brand Kit should define how the organization wants content to feel.

I am thinking it should guide:

- Brand voice.
- Visual style.
- Approved colors.
- Preferred wording.
- Product or service positioning.
- Campaign direction.
- Brand project differences.

The Brand Kit should make generation, captions, templates, and campaign output more consistent. For teams, this matters because different people may create content, but the public brand should still feel unified.

## Admin Members

The Members page should control who belongs to the organization.

It should help admins:

- Invite new members.
- See current members.
- Assign roles.
- Limit members to certain brand projects when needed.
- Adjust permission exceptions.
- Remove or revoke access when needed.

This page is important because team collaboration only works if access is controlled clearly.

## Admin Roles

The Roles page should define what different types of members can do.

I am thinking roles should answer questions like:

- Who can create drafts?
- Who can approve content?
- Who can publish?
- Who can schedule?
- Who can manage assets?
- Who can invite members?
- Who can manage channels?
- Who needs final approval before publishing?

Roles should prevent every permission decision from being handled one person at a time.

## Admin Pipelines

The Pipelines page should define how content moves through review.

I am thinking an admin should be able to design workflows such as:

- Draft submitted.
- Copy review.
- Design review.
- Manager approval.
- Client approval.
- Final approval.
- Ready to schedule.

Different organizations may need different review paths. A small business might need one approval step. An agency may need internal review and client review.

The Pipeline configuration should shape what happens after a draft leaves My Office.

## Admin Credits

The Credits page should help the organization understand and manage usage.

I am thinking credits could apply to AI generation, AI assistance, or other usage-based actions.

The page should help admins see:

- How many credits the organization has.
- How credits are being used.
- Which members may need limits.
- Whether someone has requested more credits.
- Whether the organization is approaching a limit.

Credit governance matters because team usage can grow quickly when many people can generate content.

## Admin Settings

Admin Settings should hold organization-wide controls.

I am thinking this area could include:

- Connected social accounts.
- Account health.
- Task status setup.
- Organization defaults.
- Publishing preferences.
- Workspace-level configuration.

This should be the place where admins manage the shared settings that affect multiple pages.

## Join and Invitation Flow

The Join flow should help a new member enter the organization cleanly.

I am thinking it should support:

- Accepting an invite.
- Creating an account if needed.
- Joining the correct organization.
- Receiving the correct role.
- Landing in the right workspace after joining.

This flow matters because a bad invite experience can block team adoption before the user ever reaches the workspace.

## Client Review Flow

The Client Review flow should support outside approval without giving the client full workspace access.

I am thinking this is useful for agencies or teams that need external feedback.

A client should be able to:

- Open a review link.
- Preview the content.
- Approve the content.
- Request changes.
- Leave feedback.

This should keep external approval simple while keeping the internal organization workspace controlled.

## End-to-End Organization Flow

The organization content lifecycle I am thinking about is:

Idea -> Draft -> Review -> Revision or Approval -> Schedule -> Publish -> Activity Record.

In more detail:

1. A member creates a draft in My Office or through an organization Generate flow.
2. The draft uses brand guidance, templates, assets, or campaign context.
3. The member submits the draft into the Pipeline.
4. Reviewers approve it, reject it, or request changes.
5. If changes are needed, the draft returns to the creator.
6. If approved, the content becomes ready for Calendar scheduling.
7. The team schedules or publishes it.
8. The activity is visible to the team.
9. Assets and decisions remain reusable for future work.

## Admin Setup Flow

The organization admin setup flow I am thinking about is:

1. Create or provision the organization.
2. Set up the Brand Kit.
3. Invite members.
4. Define roles.
5. Create approval pipelines.
6. Connect social accounts.
7. Configure task statuses and defaults.
8. Monitor credits and account health.

This setup makes the daily workspace usable for members.

## Member Daily Flow

The member daily flow I am thinking about is:

1. Open the Workspace dashboard.
2. Check assigned tasks, drafts, and review pressure.
3. Create or continue a draft in My Office.
4. Use assets, templates, and brand guidance.
5. Submit content to the Pipeline.
6. Respond to revision requests.
7. Help move approved content into the Calendar.
8. Use Common Room when collaboration is needed.

The goal is for a member to know what to do next without chasing context across different tools.

## Reviewer Flow

The reviewer flow I am thinking about is:

1. Open Pipeline or Workspace dashboard.
2. See items waiting for review.
3. Preview the content.
4. Approve, reject, or request changes.
5. Add clear feedback.
6. Move approved content toward scheduling.

This flow should make review decisions visible and traceable.

## Scheduler or Publisher Flow

The scheduler or publisher flow I am thinking about is:

1. Open Calendar.
2. Review approved content waiting to be placed.
3. Choose dates and platforms.
4. Confirm account readiness.
5. Schedule posts or publish immediately.
6. Track what has gone out and what is still pending.

This role may be separate from the creator or reviewer in larger organizations.

## Collaboration Flow

The collaboration flow I am thinking about is:

1. A team member starts a discussion in Common Room.
2. They reference an asset, draft, campaign, or pipeline item.
3. Other members comment or make decisions.
4. The discussion informs the content workflow.
5. Important context stays connected to the work.

The goal is to reduce scattered conversations and keep collaboration close to the content.

## Asset Reuse Flow

The asset reuse flow I am thinking about is:

1. A member uploads or finds an asset in the library.
2. The asset is organized into the right folder or brand project.
3. The asset is selected for a draft, post, or calendar item.
4. The team can later see where that asset was used.
5. High-performing assets can be reused in future campaigns.

The Asset Library should become more valuable as the organization creates more content.

## Permission and Governance Idea

Permissions should protect the organization without making the product hard to use.

I am thinking permissions should control:

- Who can invite members.
- Who can create or edit roles.
- Who can create drafts.
- Who can approve content.
- Who can publish directly.
- Who can schedule content.
- Who can manage the asset library.
- Who can manage connected accounts.
- Who can configure pipelines.

The user should not need to understand the full permission system. They should simply see the actions they are allowed to take.

## Possible Final Outcomes

The organization workspace could produce several outcomes:

- A new team member joins the workspace.
- A draft is created.
- A draft is submitted for review.
- A reviewer approves or requests changes.
- A task is assigned or completed.
- An asset is uploaded or reused.
- A post is scheduled.
- A post is published.
- A client approves or requests changes.
- An admin updates roles, members, pipelines, credits, brand rules, or settings.

The simplest way to explain the full organization concept is:

The organization workspace helps a team turn ideas into approved, scheduled, and published social content while keeping roles, assets, brand rules, approvals, collaboration, and account settings in one place.
